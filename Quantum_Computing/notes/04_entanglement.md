# Entanglement

For composite systems, the state space is the tensor product ℋ_A ⊗ ℋ_B.

## Product vs Entangled States
A product state has the form |ψ⟩_A ⊗ |φ⟩_B.
An entangled state cannot be written as such a product.

## Bell States
A canonical example:
|Φ⁺⟩ = (|00⟩ + |11⟩)/√2.

These states exhibit perfect correlations.

## Reduced States
Taking the partial trace over one subsystem yields a mixed state, even if the global state is pure.

## Operational Meaning
Measurements on one subsystem affect correlations with the other, though no faster-than-light signaling occurs.

## Relevance
Entanglement is a key resource for quantum communication and protocols like E91 QKD.
